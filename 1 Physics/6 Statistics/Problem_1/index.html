üìò Exploring the Central Limit Theorem through Simulations üéØ Motivation The Central Limit Theorem (CLT) is one of the foundational results in probability theory. It states: If \( X_1, X_2, \dots, X_n \) are i.i.d. with mean \( \mu \) and variance \( \sigma^2 \), then as \( n \to \infty \):

[ \frac{\bar{X}_n - \mu}{\sigma / \sqrt{n}} \xrightarrow{d} \mathcal{N}(0, 1) ] In simpler terms, regardless of the shape of the original distribution, the sampling distribution of the sample mean becomes approximately normal if the sample size is large enough.
